Spaghetti al Limone

Spaghetti al Limone: a beautiful, refreshing recipe for (not only) the summertime!

Ingredients

  • 500g spghaetti or more, depending on appetite
  • 5 slices of preserved lemon or zest of 1 organic lemon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 80g Parmesan cheese
  • 2tbsp crème fraîche
  • 1 handful of finely chopped parsley
  • Freshly grounded white pepper (whole)
  • 1 handful of arugula leaves
  • 2tbsp extra fine olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. As always, the sauce should wait for the pasta. However, since the sauce is so quick to make, we'll start with the pasta. So: bring a large pot of water (at least 4 liters) to a boil.
  2. Then, add a handful of salt. The water should be as salty as the sea in summer.
  3. Add the spaghetti and stir well after a minute to prevent them from sticking together. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions—or for as long as you like them.
  4. Roughly chop the preserved lemon slices. Or, if using an organic lemon, wash it thoroughly with hot water and zest the peel.
  5. Place all ingredients except for the parsley, arugula, and olive oil into a food processor (). Alternatively, use an immersion blender (). Blend everything for 2-3 minutes until the sauce is smooth and free of large chunks.

Lemon Sauce Ingredients

  1. Season with salt and white pepper. Black pepper works too, but it will leave dark specks in the light sauce.

Blending the Lemon Sauce

  1. Just before the pasta is done, set aside about 200 ml of the cooking water. Drain the pasta in a colander (*), letting it drip briefly. Then return the pasta to the warm pot or transfer it to a pre-warmed bowl.
  2. Quickly mix in the sauce. Then fold in the chopped parsley and arugula leaves. If the pasta absorbs too much liquid, add a bit of the reserved cooking water.
  3. Finally, drizzle some good olive oil over the top in a fine stream. Serve immediately.
